An In-Depth Look at My Roles During BSA
  1. Junior Assistant Scout Master
    • Mentored the new Senior Patrol Leader of my troop to lead the troop.
    • Continued to gain experience communicating with adult leaders and scouts alike.
  2. Crestwoods Park Informal Trail Restoration Eagle Scout Project
    • Led my Eagle Scout project in Crestwoods Park in which all planning and communication was based on me.
    • Direct communication with the Kirkland Parks department during the planning and adapting to their needs on the fly to what they expected to have as a finalized project were essential skills needed to complete the project in time and to have a satisfactory product.
    • On-site skills that were essential would be the ability to lead and coordinate a group, teaching everyone in the said group to follow outlined steps to reach a certain goal by the end of the workday.
  3. Senior Patrol Leader
    • Was the Senior Patrol Leader for Troop 615 which included working together with the Scoutmaster to plan, set up, and conduct troop meetings and outings.
    • Gained experience in writing many professional emails to adults and took responsibility for the well-being of the troop during outings.
    • During the pandemic oversaw setting up group zoom meetings and contacting merit badge counselors to present and work with scouts through merit badges.
